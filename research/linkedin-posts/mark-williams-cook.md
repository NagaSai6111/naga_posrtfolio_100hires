# Post 1 – Reddit Is a Community, Not an SEO Strategy

**Approx. Date:** Recent

## Summary

Mark argues that brands should avoid flooding Reddit with AI-generated promotional content simply because Reddit appears frequently in AI search results. Communities value authentic participation, and forced brand engagement can damage trust instead of building it.

## Key Insights

- Reddit users reject obvious AI-generated marketing.
- Community engagement should be authentic.
- AI should assist monitoring and research rather than mass posting.
- Long-term brand trust is more valuable than short-term visibility.

## Why It Matters for AI SEO

AI tools can help identify discussions, sentiment, and opportunities, but businesses should focus on adding genuine value rather than automating participation.

# Post 2 – AI Is Much Bigger Than Large Language Models

**Approx. Date:** Recent

## Summary

Mark explains that many marketers incorrectly use the term "AI" to mean only ChatGPT or other Large Language Models (LLMs). He highlights that LLMs are only one branch of AI, which also includes machine learning, deep learning, computer vision, and many other technologies.

## Key Insights

- AI is broader than generative AI.
- LLMs are only one subset of artificial intelligence.
- Understanding AI fundamentals leads to better business decisions.
- Marketers should avoid oversimplifying AI concepts.

## Why It Matters for AI SEO

Knowing the broader AI landscape helps SEO professionals choose the right technology for automation, analytics, personalization, and content optimization.

# Post 3 – Measuring Visibility in AI Search Requires Better Metrics

**Approx. Date:** Recent

## Summary

Mark discusses why counting citations alone is not an accurate way to measure visibility in AI-generated search results. A brand may be mentioned extensively without receiving formal citations, meaning businesses should evaluate overall visibility rather than relying on a single metric.

## Key Insights

- Citations do not represent complete visibility.
- AI search introduces new measurement challenges.
- Brand mentions are often as important as hyperlinks.
- SEO metrics must evolve with AI search.

## Why It Matters for AI SEO

Traditional SEO reporting is changing as AI-generated answers become more common. Businesses need broader measurement frameworks.

# Post 5 – Build Prompt Libraries Like Market Research

**Approx. Date:** Recent

## Summary

Mark recommends treating prompt libraries as structured research datasets instead of random collections of prompts. Businesses should organize prompts around customer journeys, products, industries, and decision stages to better evaluate AI visibility.

## Key Insights

- Prompt libraries require organization.
- Track prompt performance by intent.
- Customer journeys should guide prompt design.
- AI visibility should be measured systematically.

## Why It Matters for AI SEO

Structured prompt libraries improve AI search research and provide more reliable insights into how brands appear across different AI platforms.

# Post 6 – Let Users Judge Your Content

**Approx. Date:** Recent

## Summary

Mark argues that marketers often rely too heavily on abstract engagement metrics when evaluating content quality. Instead, he recommends asking users directly whether an article was helpful and using that feedback to improve future content.

## Key Insights

- User feedback is the most valuable quality signal.
- Engagement metrics alone are insufficient.
- Collect explicit quality ratings.
- Improve content based on real audience feedback.

## Why It Matters for AI SEO

As AI-generated content becomes more common, user satisfaction signals become increasingly valuable for improving content quality and long-term search performance.
